I'm a Master's student in Electrical Engineering at the University of Rhode Island, working on computer vision, multimodal sensor fusion, and machine learning for clinical movement assessment.

My current research spans vision-language models for ingestive-behavior coaching (NIH R01 — DIBS), EEG/motion-capture fusion for movement decoding and Brain-Computer Interfaces, automated neuromuscular assessment with socially assistive robots, and tripolar concentric ring electrode (TCRE) EEG. I work primarily in Python with PyTorch, Hugging Face, OpenCV, and MNE.

Before joining ECBE, I trained as an exercise scientist (M.S., Shahid Beheshti University; B.S. Sports Engineering, Islamic Azad University) and worked on wearable inertial sensor validation, EMG/biomechanics in ALS, and physiological signal analysis. That background still shapes how I think about translating measurement systems into something a clinician can actually use.

If you'd like to chat about research, collaborations, or anything in the overlap of ML and biomedical signals, the best way to reach me is by [email](mailto:skhodabakhsh@uri.edu).
